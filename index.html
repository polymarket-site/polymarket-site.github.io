What is Polymarket?

Polymarket is a prediction-market app for trading event outcomes, with an international CLOB that matches orders offchain and settles matched trades on Polygon. Its official trading docs describe EIP-712 signed orders, non-custodial trading, and onchain settlement.

How Polymarket works with USDC on Polygon

Polymarket is a prediction market where users buy and sell outcome shares tied to real-world events. Markets are typically collateralized with USDC on Polygon, and prices can be read as market-implied probabilities before fees, spreads, and liquidity effects. A share that resolves correctly pays out according to the market rules; an incorrect outcome becomes worthless. Users searching how Polymarket works should focus on the market question, resolution source, end date, order book liquidity, and whether the outcome wording matches their intended view.

Polymarket fees, spreads, and liquidity

Polymarket trading costs are not only explicit fees. The practical cost is often the bid-ask spread, market depth, price impact, and any funding or withdrawal cost for getting USDC onto Polygon. Liquid markets such as major politics, sports, crypto, or macro events usually have tighter spreads than niche markets. Before buying a large position, users should inspect the order book, available shares at each price, potential slippage, and rules for resolution. A market showing high volume can still have thin liquidity at the exact price a user wants.

Polymarket withdrawals and deposits

Polymarket deposits and withdrawals revolve around supported wallet and USDC flows on Polygon or integrated funding methods offered in the app. Users should verify the exact chain and token before sending funds, because USDC exists on multiple networks and not every deposit address or payment method accepts every version. Withdrawal timing depends on the selected rail, wallet transaction confirmation, and any compliance or account checks required by the app. For self-custody flows, keeping POL or the required gas token available can prevent stuck wallet transactions.

Polymarket market resolution and disputes

Polymarket markets resolve according to their written rules, resolution source, and oracle or dispute process. Searchers often ask because the headline question can feel obvious while the fine print defines the actual outcome. Before trading, read the rules for time zone, source hierarchy, ambiguous cases, and cancellation conditions. A market can be controversial if news develops after close, if a source revises information, or if the wording excludes a scenario users assumed was included. The market description is more important than social-media interpretation.

Polymarket FAQ

Does Polymarket have a token?

Polymarket does not have a public platform token used for ordinary trading. Markets are generally collateralized with USDC, and users should be cautious about fake Polymarket token claims or airdrop links unless they are confirmed through Polymarket's official site or social channels.

What network does Polymarket use?

Polymarket is crypto-native and has used Polygon for market settlement and USDC collateral flows. Users should confirm the network inside the live app before depositing, because sending the wrong USDC version or using the wrong chain can create recovery problems.

How are Polymarket odds calculated?

Polymarket odds come from market prices where traders buy and sell outcome shares. A Yes share trading near 60 cents is often read as roughly 60% implied probability, but spreads, liquidity, fees, and market rules mean it is not a pure forecast or guaranteed probability.

Can U.S. users trade on Polymarket?

Access depends on Polymarket's current terms, jurisdiction rules, and user eligibility checks. Because prediction market rules are regulatory-sensitive and can change, users should rely on the live Polymarket terms and onboarding flow rather than old guides or social posts.

What happens when a Polymarket market resolves?

When a market resolves, winning outcome shares become redeemable according to the market's payout rules and losing shares become worthless. The exact process depends on the market, oracle, and app workflow. Users should keep track of market status and claim or redeem when available.

Why is Polymarket price different from polls?

Polymarket prices reflect traders risking capital, not a direct poll of public opinion. Prices can react to polling, news, liquidity, hedging demand, and trader positioning. A prediction market can disagree with polls because it incorporates different incentives, assumptions, and information sources.
